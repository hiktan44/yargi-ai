# Public Domain Legal Case Sources for Truth Re:Build AI

## Overview
This document provides verified public domain and freely accessible legal case databases that can be safely used for educational and demonstration purposes in your hackathon project.

## Primary Legal Databases

### 1. BAILII (British and Irish Legal Information Institute)
- **URL**: https://www.bailii.org/
- **Access**: Completely free
- **Coverage**: British and Irish case law & legislation, European Union case law
- **Status**: Public domain legal information
- **Terms**: Free access for everyone, donations supported
- **Content Available**:
  - UK case law (England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland)
  - Irish case law
  - European Union case law
  - Law Commission reports
  - Legal legislation

### 2. Hong Kong Legal Information Institute (HKLII)  
- **URL**: https://www.hklii.hk/
- **Access**: Free access to Hong Kong legal information
- **Coverage**: Hong Kong judgments and legislation
- **Status**: Public access database
- **Content Available**:
  - Hong Kong court judgments
  - Hong Kong legislation
  - Legal sentencing predictor tools

### 3. Hong Kong Judiciary Official Database
- **URL**: https://www.judiciary.hk/en/judgments_legal_reference/judgments.html
- **Access**: Direct free access to official judgments
- **Coverage**: Hong Kong court judgments
- **Status**: Official government database - public access
- **Content Available**:
  - Current judgments
  - Newly added judgments (last 6 working days)
  - Reasons for verdicts and sentences

### 4. Additional Free Legal Databases
- **CaseLaw Access Project**: https://case.law/ (US cases)
- **Justia Free Case Law**: https://law.justia.com/cases/ (US federal and state cases)
- **Google Scholar**: https://scholar.google.com/schhp?hl=en&as_sdt=0,5&as_vis=1 (Academic legal research)
- **WorldLII**: http://www.worldlii.org/ (Global legal database)

## Usage Guidelines for Hackathon Demo

### ✅ SAFE TO USE
- Cases from BAILII, HKLII, and Hong Kong Judiciary databases
- Cases clearly marked as public domain
- Historical cases older than copyright terms
- Cases with explicit free usage permissions
- Academic citations and references to public cases

### ⚠️ RECOMMENDED PRACTICES
- **Always anonymize**: Replace real names with "Witness A", "Defendant B", etc.
- **Add disclaimers**: "Simulated data for educational purposes, names changed for privacy; not legal advice"
- **Use historical cases**: Focus on older, well-established precedent cases
- **Truncate sensitive details**: Remove personal information, addresses, specific medical details

### ❌ AVOID
- Recent controversial cases still in appeal
- Cases involving minors or sensitive personal data
- Proprietary legal research databases without permission
- Cases from subscription-only services

## Specific Case Recommendations for Demo

### Negligence Cases (Public Domain)
- **Donoghue v Stevenson (1932)** - Classic negligence case (UK)
- **Caparo Industries plc v Dickman (1990)** - Three-part test for duty of care (UK)
- Historical Hong Kong negligence precedents from HKLII

### Contract Breach Cases
- **Carlill v Carbolic Smoke Ball Co (1893)** - Famous contract case (UK)
- **Hadley v Baxendale (1854)** - Damages in contract law (UK)

### Safe Demo Data Strategy
1. **Base Structure**: Use real legal precedent structure and reasoning
2. **Anonymize Details**: Change all names, locations, specific details
3. **Educational Purpose**: Clear disclaimer about educational/demo use
4. **Historical Focus**: Prioritize established, older cases from public databases

## Legal Safety Disclaimer
This research focuses on public domain and freely accessible legal information for educational demonstration purposes. All case details should be anonymized, and the application should include appropriate disclaimers stating that the content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice.
